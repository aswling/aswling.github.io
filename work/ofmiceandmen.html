Of Mice and Men

Skills: Illustration, print design, book design

Tools: Illustrator, Photoshop

Final assignment for Design II at NYU in Spring 2017. Special thanks to Professor Theresa Fitzgerald for her guidance throughout the semester.

The assignment was to redesign the book cover for a classic piece of literature. I chose one of my favorite books from high school, John Steinbeck’s Of Mice and Men. I wanted to update the cover to appeal to more contemporary audiences while still reminiscent of design from the 1930s. Thus, I began researching Works Projects Administration (WPA) advertisements. The WPA employed artists and designers for public projects, including posters to create a sense of national pride and encourage Americans to explore the breadth of natural beauty throughout the country. Amidst the economic and social deterioration from the Great Depression, WPA felt art could enrich the lives of Americans and be accessible to all.

After looking at actual WPA posters and vintage-inspired designs from the Anderson Design Group, I started sketching illustrations and possible type choices for the final redesign. I ultimately decided to stick to a limited neutral color palette and bold sans-serif type. The final cover depicts two rabbits, representative of protagonist and deuteragonist, George and Lennie, sitting in front of the camping spot in which the final scene of the novella occurs.
